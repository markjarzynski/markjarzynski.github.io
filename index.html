Projects & Research

Ray Tracing for Accelerated Metrology

Using Nvidia Optix ray tracing to simulate electron micropsy.
https://github.com/markjarzynski/RTAM

EGISImporter

Epic MegaGrant for importing EGIS data (shape files) into Unreal Engine as a point cloud.
https://github.com/ircumbc/EGISImporter

Losing Winter

AR mobile app to experience and share vlogs of memories of snow and the winter.
On display until July 2022 at the Maryland Center for History and Culture
losingwinter.net
iOS Android

Hash Functions for GPU Rendering.

Evaluated existing hash functions for random number quality using TestU01 and GPU execution speed through benchmarking.
Paper: http://jcgt.org/published/0009/03/02/
Shadertoy: https://www.shadertoy.com/view/XlGcRh
Presented at I3D 2021: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dwi5qJ6oPjE&t=7958s

Virtual Buffet

A virtual dining experience where participants would select foods while wearing an EKG. Virtual measurements and results were compared to real-life measurements and results.
https://www.irc.umbc.edu/2019/07/17/dining-at-the-virtual-buffet

Ecomimesis

VR experience of 3D photogrammetry scanned plants. Exhibited at Hustle at the Science Lab Gallery Detroit.
https://www.irc.umbc.edu/projects/plantelligence

BEARINGS of Baltimore, Circa 1815

Interactive touch screen display of a digitally recreated 3D map of Baltimore, Maryland circa 1815. On pernament display at the Maryland Historical Society
https://earlybaltimore.org/

Cranes in Motion

Interactive 3D application utilizing Microsoft Kinect for motion controlling a 3D whooping crane. On pernament display at the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service Patuxent Visitor Center
https://www.irc.umbc.edu/projects/cranes-in-motion

Dome Explorer

iPad app that explores the history of science through an augmented reality tour of the Great Hall ceiling at the National Academy of Sciences building in Washington, D.C. On permanent display at the National Academy of Sciences.
http://nasgreathall.com
https://www.irc.umbc.edu/projects/dome-explorer
