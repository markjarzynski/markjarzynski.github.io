I'm seeking Graphics Engineering positions within the games industry, either directly working on a title or on a shared game engine. I prefer to be in an office in the Baltimore-D.C.-Annapolis area, but willing to work remotely. I have over 15 years of experience in software development, five of which directly in games, ten games-adjacent. I have three published papers, one of which is highly referenced in the field. I'm a part time Ph.D. student, admitted to candidacy in 2024. My dissertation is in hash functions on the GPU, specifically looking at performance and quality, as well how the choice of hash impacts the visual quality of the things that use them.

I enjoy working closely with artists and tech artists, to make sure they have the tech and tools to make their vision come to reality. I have mentored others and enjoy teaching. I always find that I learn something better when I have to teach or explain it. I am self-directed and I can fill in the blanks when given an incomplete jira ticket, working with production to make sure the required tasks are pointed and tasked out.

I like debugging and optimizing shaders, using tools like PIX, Nvidia Nsight Aftermath, and Radeon Developer Tool Suite to profile and find and fix issues. I also like to optimize code and write SSE instructions.

Games

Ashes of the Singularity II

Large-scale RTS. Maintained and developed graphics features and shaders. Rewrote the Terrain shaders with stochastic hex tiling in mind and created tools for artist to control the terrain textures and how they blend. Added support for area lights to Nitrous and did a lot of point light optimization. Added non-PBR techniques, such as fake hair lighting for the artist to attach to units and made it so team color doesn't get colored by directional lights and stays true. Modified the tone curve to optionally preserve hue, such that reds don't turn orange when getting hot. Fixed several issues with our CSG algorithms used for player borders. Wrote parallel and optimized code, used SSE where appropirate.

Sins of a Solar Empire II: Paths to Power

Real-time 4X space strategy game. I fixed many graphics bugs that existed in the base game, and improved performance on both the GPU and CPU in key places. Implemented the lua scripting, and scripted many of the scenarios in in Paths to Power, keeping it mod-friendly.

Robot Teleoperation with Virtual Reality

Study to determine human-robot interaction in VR by simulating a husky robot in ROS and steaming it's point-cloud data into Unreal Engine via EGISImporter. Operators were able to view the robot and it's sensor data through a VR headset and control the robot with a controller. Operators were tasked with operating the robot through a maze in both VR and via traditional 2D display and controller. The timings on completing the maze were recorded, and operators were asked to self report which experience they thought was easier to control the robot in.
https://github.com/markjarzynski/VRTeleoperation
